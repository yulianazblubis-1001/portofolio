Rey.id Insurtech 2021-2023

Building a Complete Claim Ecosystem from Scratch

As PM, I owned the entire claim experience across both member-facing app and internal processing tools. Over 2+ years, I grew from PM to Senior PM, shipping 5 interconnected products that transformed how health insurance claims work in Indonesia.

Context

Rey.id was a health insurtech startup disrupting Indonesia's insurance market. When I joined, the vision was clear: make health insurance seamless for members and profitable for the company.

I owned the entire claim experience—both what customers see (the mobile app) and what the internal team operates (the claims processing tool). This wasn't a bug; it was the secret. Over 2+ years, I grew from Product Manager to Senior PM, launching 5 products that together formed a complete claim ecosystem. My growth track was tied to the business growth: as we scaled, the tools I built needed to scale with us.

The Problem

The Chicken-and-Egg Problem

Rey.id needed a large hospital network to attract members, but hospitals only wanted to partner with insurers who already had many customers. Catch-22.

Two interconnected sub-problems:

Member Side (The App)

  • • Pay upfront at hospital, wait days for reimbursement
  • • Separate menus for outpatient vs inpatient claims (members don't think in categories)
  • • Manual document uploads and slow claim processing

Internal Side (The Claims Team)

  • • ReyCare team: ~5 claims per shift + 20 chats simultaneously
  • • One giant form with no workflow structure
  • • Slow processing = frustrated members = bad customer experience

Key Insight:

What happens in the internal tool directly reflects in the member's app. A slow, error-prone claims process inside = a frustrated customer outside. You can't separate the two. I had to fix the engine before we could paint the car.

Key Decision #1: How to Break the Chicken-and-Egg

Three options. Each with clear trade-offs.

Wait for Hospital Partnerships

Hope big hospitals will sign with us before we have customers.

Why rejected:

Too slow. Hospitals move at enterprise speed. We'd run out of runway.

Reimbursement-Only Model

Let members pay upfront, process claims in the app.

Why rejected:

Bad UX. "Pay now, maybe get money back later" isn't compelling.

Own Claim Team + ReyCard

Build internal claim processing team. Issue debit card (ReyCard) so members never pay upfront.

Why chosen:

Higher cost but broke the chicken-and-egg immediately. Speed became our competitive advantage. Members felt zero friction.

Trade-off made: Higher unit economics in the short term, but buying time to scale. This decision rippled through everything else.

Key Decision #2: What to Build First

Once we committed to owning the claim process, the question became: internal tools first, or member app first?

Internal Tool First

Redesign the claims processing workflow for our team. Fix the engine before you paint the car.

Why chosen:

Right answer was counterintuitive. Fix internal processes first, and the member experience improves automatically.

Design Principles for Internal Tool

🎯 Clear & Transparent: Every field, every calculation, every decision visible. No hidden rules.

📊 Informatively Minimal: Show only what's needed at each step. Reduce cognitive load.

⚙️ Simple to Operate: Minimal keystrokes. Inline editing. Fast navigation. Aim for 15-min SLA.

How we thought about it: HMW reduce cognitive load so our team can process claims faster AND reduce member anxiety by processing claims within SLA?

The Ecosystem I Built (5 Products)

1. Claim Internal Tools (InTools)

Web app for ReyCare team. Redesigned the chaotic 6-phase claim process into 6 clean pages. PDF viewer, auto-mapped expense groups, inline-editable tables. Target SLA: 15 min per claim.

  • ✓ Pre-admission → Admission → Monitoring → Payment flow
  • ✓ Smart form auto-population from documents
  • ✓ Inline editing (no save button = faster workflow)

2. ReyCard

Indonesia's first health insurtech debit card. Partnership with Bank Sampoerna & Xendit for payment processing. Card auth connects directly to claim assessment system.

  • ✓ Zero friction at hospital (no upfront payment required)
  • ✓ Claim assessed at point of transaction
  • ✓ Breaking the chicken-and-egg in one product

3. Outpatient & Inpatient Menu (Mobile)

Merged what used to be two separate menus into one unified CRUD system. Members don't think in categories. Document upload, GPS tracking, QRIS payment integration.

  • ✓ Unified claim submission experience
  • ✓ Easy document upload with photo verification
  • ✓ Real-time claim status tracking

4. Emergency Menu

911 for members. GPS-enabled ambulance dispatch, real-time chat & call with ReyCare team. Instant hospital referral.

  • ✓ Panic button for members in crisis
  • ✓ Geo-routing to nearest hospital
  • ✓ Pre-authorization while in transit

5. ML Fraud Detection + e-KYC

Auto-flagging suspicious claims. Integration with VIDA (facial recognition) and OCR for document verification. Reduced fraud by 60%.

  • ✓ Real-time claim validation
  • ✓ Automated KYC via biometrics
  • ✓ Reduced manual review overhead

Key Decision #3: Merging Outpatient & Inpatient

Members submitted claims for outpatient and inpatient separately. Two menus. Two forms. But members don't think in categories—they just think "I got sick, I need to claim."

Keep Separate

Maintain distinct outpatient vs inpatient flows.

Why rejected:

Members are forced to choose a category they don't understand. More friction. More support queries.

Merge into One CRUD System

One claim form. Smart routing based on claim type.

Why chosen:

Higher upfront cost to rebuild, but simpler mental model. Members get it immediately. Support tickets dropped by 40%.

Impact: We reduced decision fatigue at a critical moment—when members need help most. That's product thinking: solve for how people actually behave, not how systems are organized.

Edge Cases: When Things Don't Work

Real product work isn't about the happy path. It's about handling the messy edge cases that make or break trust.

🏥 "Card Declined at Hospital"

What if the member tries to use ReyCard and it gets declined? Panic. Embarrassment. At a hospital.

Solution: Instant QRIS fallback. Hospital scans QR code instead. Claim flagged for immediate ReyCare review. Member gets SMS: "Your claim is being processed. You'll pay nothing if approved."

🔍 "Monitoring Phase Complexity"

Some claims span multiple days with multiple encounters. The monitoring phase can become a nightmare—claims agents manually calculating totals, introducing errors.

Solution: Auto-calculation tables in InTools. Rows update in real-time. Agents can't make math errors if math is automatic. Result: 40% fewer calculation mistakes. More claim approvals.

What I Learned

Think in Systems, Not Features

A feature is a single thing. A system is how everything connects. Claim processing isn't one product—it's 5 products talking to each other. Understanding the whole > nailing one part.

Internal Tools Need Advocacy

Nobody glamorizes an internal tool. But it's where the magic happens. I had to fight to protect time for the claims team's experience. That was the leverage point for everything else.

Medical Domains Have Hidden Rules

You can't guess how claims work. You need to sit with doctors, nurses, and claims agents. Understand the workflows. Don't apply generic product logic—respect domain expertise.
